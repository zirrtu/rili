1. Abstract

This paper presents a singing voice conversion (SVC) system, denoted as T02, for the Singing Voice Conversion Challenge 2023 (SVCC). Our system entails a VITS-based SVC system, mainly incorporating three modules: a feature extractor, a conversion module, and a post-processing module. An initial step involves the feature extractor extracting linguistic content features from a singing signal. Subsequently, a VITS-based conversion model is employed to generate the waveform of the target speaker. Besides, to reduce artifacts generated by the conversion model, a fine-tuned DSPGAN is introduced to re-synthesis waveform after conversion. Since the data of the target singer is limited to roughly 10 minutes, we follow the pretraining-adaptation schema, in which we pre-train SVC model on speech and singing data sequentially and adapt SVC model to the target singer. To achieve better adaptation performance, we also implement several tricks, such as data augmentation and joint training with auxiliary singer data. Note that we employ the same implementation for both in-domain and cross-domain tasks of SVCC 2023. The evaluation results of SVCC 2023 affirm the performance of our system in terms of naturalness and similarity.
